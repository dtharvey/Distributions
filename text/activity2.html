The Poisson Distribution: Emission of Beta Particles from 40K

The figure on the right is a barplot that shows the number of beta particles emitted by a sample of 40K during 365 consecutive 7-s intervals. In some intervals, no beta particles were emitted; in other intervals, as many as 13 beta particles were emitted.

Exercise 1. Although a barplot and a histogram look similar, they are not interchangeable. A histogram is useful for continuous data and a barplot is useful for discrete data. Why are the results of beta emission considered discrete instead of continuous?
