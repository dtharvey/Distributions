Copper/Sulfur Ratio in Copper Sulfide. If you mix powdered copper metal with a stoichiometric excess of powdered sulfur and ignite them in a crucible, copper (I) sulfide forms with an empirical formula of CuxS. The value of x is determined by weighing the Cu before ignition and then determining the mass of CuxS when the reaction is complete; any excess sulfur is removed as \(\require{mhchem} \ce{SO2}\). The plot in the upper-left corner shows the distribution of the values of x for 62 trials.

Emission of Beta Particles from 40K. In beta emission, an unstable neutron decays into an electron and a proton. The proton remains in the nucleus, which increases the atomâ€™s atomic number by one; the electron is ejected as a beta particle. The plot in the upper-right corner shows the distribution of the number of beta particles emitted from a single sample of 40K during each of 365 consecutive 7-s intervals.

Certification of Class A 10-mL Pipettes. A class A pipette delivers a calibrated volume of liquid. Each pipet has a unique identification number and carries a certificate that states its precise calibrated volume traced to a NIST-approved standard. The plot in the lower-left corner shows the distribution of calibrations for 1000 Class A 10-mL pipettes.

13C Atoms in Cholesterol. Carbon has two stable isotopes: 12C and 13C. Because 12C is much more abundant than 13C, most organic molecules have no atoms of 13C; a few molecules, however, will contain one or more atoms of 13C. The plot in the lower-right corner shows the distribution of the number of 13C atoms in 100 molecules of cholesterol.
